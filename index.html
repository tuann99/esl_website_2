What we do

The MSU Exposure Science Laboratory incorporates measurement and analytical tools in order to identify the sources of physical, chemical, and biological hazards and their potential exposure and mitigation pathways in ecological, occupational, community, and personal settings. We work with investigators in federal/local governments and academia (e.g. University of Michigan, University of New Mexico) in diverse disciplines including medicine, engineering, and toxicology, to just name a few. We always welcome new collaboration opportunities!

Services we offer

We provide sampling and analysis services and expert consultation.

Our facility includes ISO 5/Class 100 clean rooms housing an array of analytical instruments (e.g. a high-resolution inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometer and a direct mercury analyzer) and is equipped to analyze clinical (e.g. blood spot), biological and environmental samples for a full spectrum of elements, with emphasis on metals and metalloids in ultratrace concentrations. If you would like to inquire about our services and pricing, please fill out this form.

High-resolution inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometer (HR-ICPMS)
We can analyze a wide variety of samples including PM2.5, blood spots, soil, water and tissue samples for toxic metals and other trace elements (covering over 40 elements in the periodic table). We welcome inquiries about other types of samples.

Direct Mercury Analyzer
We can process most solid, liquid and gas samples to quantify mercury.

Environmental Monitoring
We assess outdoor and indoor air quality and investigate the impacts of major sources of air pollutions using source apportionment modeling.
